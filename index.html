Sir Alex Ferguson

The man who dominated English football for three decades

A Timeline of Greatness

  • 1986 - Becomes Manchester United manager, replacing Ron Atkinson.
  • 1990 - Wins his first trophy with United, the FA Cup, against Crystal Palace.
  • 1991 - Secures the European Cup Winners' Cup, defeating Barcelona 2-1 in the final.
  • 1992 - Signs Eric Cantona, a pivotal figure in Manchester United's success.
  • 1993 - Wins Manchester United's first English Premier League title.
  • 1993 - Voted Manager of the Year.
  • 1994 - Achieves the first EPL-FA Cup double in history, retaining the English title.
  • 1995 - Appointed Commander of the Order of the British Empire (CBE).
  • 1996 - Wins the Premier League with "Fergie's Fledglings" or "Class of '92" â€“ including Beckham, Giggs, Scholes, and Neville.
  • 1997 - Secures a fourth title in five years, winning back-to-back EPL titles.
  • 1999 - Achieves a historic Premier League-FA Cup-Champions League "Treble," becoming a knight and winning the International Cup.
  • 2000 - United wins their sixth Premier League title.
  • 2002 - Reverses his decision to retire at the end of the season.
  • 2003 - Faces legal action regarding racehorse ownership; reportedly kicks a boot into Beckham's face; signs Cristiano Ronaldo.
  • 2007 - Wins the Premier League for the ninth time, denying Chelsea a third straight title.
  • 2008 - Wins the Champions League for the second time, defeating Chelsea in a penalty shootout, securing an EPL "Double"; also wins the Club World Cup.
  • 2009 - Wins a third consecutive league title and the League Cup; Manchester United reaches the Champions League final, losing to Barcelona.
  • 2010 - Wins his fourth and final League Cup.
  • 2011 - The North Stand at Old Trafford is renamed the Sir Alex Ferguson Stand.
  • 2012 - Signs Robin Van Persie, who proves instrumental.
  • 2013 - Wins a record 20th English Premier League title for Manchester United and his 13th EPL title.
  • 2013 - Retires from football management.
  • 2013 - Publishes his second autobiography, "My Autobiography."
"Best Premier League manager of all time." -- BBC
